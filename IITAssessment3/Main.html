Why We Love Vintage?

Up until recently, technological advancements have always been an improvement to their predesessors. What's the point in developing something new if it isn't better than what we already have, right?

But if that were true, why do I (and so many other people, both young and old) have a vinyl turntable? A CD player? MP3 players?

Where we are at now.

The primary method for music consumptio in the current day is through streaming services such as Spotify, Apple Music or Tidal. Users pay a monthly fee, and get access to a large library of music to stream from the services servers. While this is convenient and easy for most users, this model has given rise to many controversies recently.

Spotify's CEO

According to an ABC article from last year, Spotify CEO Daniel Ek has made significant investments into Helsing, a German military technology company currently developing artificial intelligence systems. For many users (myself included), supporting a business that supports such technology is not something they are comfortable with.

"To corrupt something that somebody has made with beautiful intent that harshly is unforgivable." - Fenn Wilson of Fenn Wilson & The Weather.

Impermanence

One drawback of streaming services includes the imperenence of their catalog. These services cannot guarantee that your favourite songs will be available forever. Plenty of artists have had their songs removed from streaming, including Fenn Wilson as mentioned above.

The Allure of Ownership

Because of reasons listed above, as well as many others, physical mediums such as Vinyl record have seen an Increase in sales over the past decade.
